Education

The Science of Freefall

Understanding the physics makes you a better flyer. Learn how aerodynamics keep you suspended in mid-air.

Lift & Drag

The upward force of the wind (lift) matches the downward force of gravity. Drag stabilizes your body.

Airflow Mechanics

Four massive fans push air through a closed loop, creating a smooth column of air in the flight chamber.

Body Positioning

Arching your back and spreading limbs creates a stable surface area, allowing you to float effortlessly.

Terminal Velocity

Speeds range from 100 to 160 mph, precisely calibrated to your body weight to achieve perfect terminal velocity.
